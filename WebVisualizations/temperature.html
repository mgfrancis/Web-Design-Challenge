Analysis of Latitude vs. Temperature

Latitude pertains to angles ranging from zero degrees at the equator to 90 degrees up to the North or down to the South poles of the earth. Temperature is inversely related to latitude. As latitude increases, temperature falls, and vice versa.

Empirically, you will find it warmer as we approach the equator and cooler as you travel towards the earth's poles. The scatter plot supports this. One can almost imagine the temperature transitioning from cold to cooler to warm or hotter, and the reverse again as we travel from one end of the pole to the other.
